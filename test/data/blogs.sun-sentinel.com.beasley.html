Time for a small step for Beasley?
> Posted by Ira Winderman on May 4, 2009 09:42 AM

            The one thing you couldn't help notice most of the season about Michael Beasley was, well, that he wasn't quite as Beast-ly as advertised.

                                                             

            Whether the assignment was Josh Smith or the other power forwards he covered, when standing side by side, Michael didn't always exactly measure up.

                                                   

            Using whatever measurement you choose, the listed 6-10 out of Kansas State, the 6-9 1/2 the Heat listed this season, the 6-8 1/4 he measured with shoes at the pre-draft camp or the 6-7 without shoes, he usually played at a size deficit at power forward.

                                           

            So why not dedicate this summer to nothing but work at small forward? Why not try to maximize this roster with Jermaine O'Neal, Udonis Haslem and Beasley across the front line next season?

                                                         

            Yes, some of the matchups will be frightening: Beasley vs. Paul Pierce, Beasley vs. LeBron, Beasley vs. Andre Iguodala. But there also is a school of thought that those guys with get their points, anyway.

                                                

            What Beasley at small forward would do is force Dwyane Wade to cover high-scoring shooting guards, no longer providing the option of flip-flopping the assignments with a defensive-minded small forward.

                                                      

            Still, in the Eastern Conference, where you play most opponents four times a season, it would appear you could survive Beasley versus what would have been this season's matchups of Marvin Williams, Luol Deng, Tayshaun Prince, Richard Jefferson, Al Harrington, Bobby Simmons, Hedo Turkoglu and Shawn Marion.

                                                

            Heck, in the Heat's final game of the season, when Beasley entered Sunday against the Hawks, he entered at small forward.

                                                    

            With significant needs in other areas, with James Jones added as a bench boost, it would be a shame for the Heat to go into 2009-10 without knowing whether Beasley at small forward isn't a legitimate option. At least take a look. It's worth finding out.
